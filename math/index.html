Mathematics

That's right, I'm one of those crazy kids who typesets all their assignments. I even upload them to the internet for your reading pleasure. I make no guarantees about the quality or accuracy of the solutions, though I typically only answer questions when I know I can answer them. Within each class they're organized by due date rather than something useful to people other than me, e.g., topics covered, page number, etc. However, you do get the source LaTeX files!

This page isn't designed to facilitate cheating, though I'm sure Professor Sally is still assigning the same problems he did my second year. Of course, if you're serious about math you wouldn't be using these to get quick answers. Then again, if you're not serious about math, you probably wouldn't be taking any of these courses.

If I had to evaluate these textbooks, I'd give high marks to the Kolmogorov and Fomin, the Edwards, and the Dummit and Foote. I would warn against the Mardsen and Hoffman — the chapters are interesting, but the exercises are almost too trivial for words. If you're going to get the Rudin you may as well save yourself the time and go straight for Real and Complex Analysis which, while more advanced, should not be beyond someone studying from Principles of Mathematical Analysis. If I had to evalutate the professors, well, the people who care know my opinions. And if you don't then you can ask me in person. Tut tut.
